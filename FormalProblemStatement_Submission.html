Problem

In the United States, the discovery phase of commercial litigation can often be the most important phase and often can quickly become the most expensive phase of the litigation life cycle. As companies begin to store and use larger amounts of data, or electronically stored information (“ESI”) and new sources of ESI continue to emerge, our organization is faced with the task of trying to efficiently manage our client’s ESI, as it relates to litigation. Managing our enterprise clients’ ESI for use in litigation, has become a firm business unit (“Client Technology Services” (“CTS”)) that now competes with many of the firm’s attorney based practice groups. Outside companies that have successfully navigated this industry over the last decade have been reporting yearly revenues over 100 million dollars (2018 Owler, Relativity).

Reed Smith’s (“RS”) CTS group has been ahead of the curve in managing our client’s ESI, demonstrated by the yearly increases in department revenue over the last 5 years, when RS began down the path to fully “in-house” this service, while many AmLaw 100 Firms continued to outsource this service to third party vendors. However, the industry is consolidating and firms are beginning to recognize this service as a large “cash-cow” (American Lawyer, “How a few Savvy Law Firms Turned eDiscovery into a Cash Cow”, 2017). RS must continue to rigorously enhance its client facing technology to stay competitive in this industry.

The emergence of alternative fee arrangements over the past years, which are predicted to increase over the coming years (2017 Litigation Trends Annual Survey, Norton Rose Fulbright), are largely a result of enterprise level general counsel, being dissatified with the services being performed by outside counsel. According to the 2016 Chief Legal Officer Survey, by Altman Weil, Inc., this dissatisfaction can largely be attributed to outside counsel’s ability to “Cost Control and Efficiency”. According to the results of the same survey, in-house general counsel’s “top efficiency initiatives are the greater use of technology tools, analysis of management metrics” and “project management training”.

The CTS department has made and continues to make significant investment in technology tools. As a result, RS has the largest amount of active Relativity(link) users in the world and recently deployed the industry leading analytics platform, Brainspace(link), to further enhance the capabilities of Relativity.

These are industry leading technologies and the CTS department does charge usage fees, to apply this technology to our clients needs, in attempt manage the collection and review of our clients’ ESI in the most efficient manner possible. However, there are a large number of uncertainties when trying to estimate the fees that can be incurred through the use of these technologies, which can dramatically affect the cost of the project.

Project Proposal

I am presenting this project to RS, in an attempt to provide a solution to our clients need to better control costs and improve the quality of the product, provided by our project management team;

If a model can accurately predict data expansion rates, documents per unit of data, given a client’s case history, estimated length of the matter and the number of potential custodians, then the model should be able to provide, within a reasonable amount of error, a projected project cost.

Project Benefits

The benefits of this project include, but at not limited to the following;

  1. Accurately present project estimates to client in client pitches, based of the client’s past matter information with RS.
  2. Present clients with metrics regarding past matters, to vizualize the firm’s commitment to monitoring client costs.
  3. Present an accurate and quantifiable metric for clients to consider when engaged in Early Discpute Resolution or settlement negotiation.
  4. Provide clients with accurate budgets, reducing the need to write down/off billable hours to stay within proposed budgets.
  5. Increase client confidence in RS abilities to efficiently manage ESI, further developing the relationship.
  6. Leverage the developed relationship to store more client data, increasing CTS department and RS revenue.
  7. Leverage client data to engage RS attorney practice groups on matters, to avoid re-collection or moving data.

Project Evaluation

Project Success

The project sets out to identify key unknown expansion rates of ESI. These are key variables in calculating fees charged for ESI storage and attorney review. The project will be evaluated as a success, if the model can predict the project cost, within a acceptable standard deviation from the historical matter project costs.

Project Risks

The risks of developing this project are minimal. The data required to complete the development of a working model already exists within firm systems. The greatest risk, would be after the deployment of the model. If certain unknown or outliers, present themself during the course of a matter, this could signifcantly affect project costs.
